Camping!

The Great Outdoors

Growing up my mom always told me that she had done enough camping in her life time and was done with it. I honestly didn't believe that she had even been camping! I tried car-camping a few times with friends and it always seemed like such a strange concept. You drive to a big park where if you're lucky a thin line of trees seperates your campsite from the next. It honestly felt worse than living in a subdivision to me. When I first met my partner neither of us had really been back-country camping, but one year he decided to buy a canoe with his friend which opened a whole new passion for both of us. The first trip we went on the three of us, me- my partner - and his friend, piled into the canoe with random bits of camp gear we though might be handy and paddled down a river to what we now call "bing-bong bay". We honestly weren't sure of where we were going or what it would be like but it was one of the best adventures to enjoy together. Since then we have been able to explore Northern Ontario and have grown to be very confident in our outdoor skills.
